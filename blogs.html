Importance of Education in Our Life

Education gives a perfect face to life, Education gives holy power to fight, Education destroys darkness with the help of light, Education gives man broad sight" Education is a powerful weapon that enables a person to face life's adversities and overcome societal stigmas such as poverty, fear, and status to achieve success. Education is the hope of most third-world and world-dominant countries for development and success. Mandatory education broadens the scope of opportunities for growth and development.

Education's Role in India
With skilled and educated youths, education has the potential to change the course of a nation. A country must achieve a higher rate of economic growth to be classified as a developed country. A well-educated nation removes any impediments to growth and works hard to achieve long-term development. The process of learning, training, and teaching human capital in schools and universities is referred to as education in India. The Indian government reflects specific economic policies that highlight the value of education in India.

Factors Influencing Education's Importance in India
Get rid of the erratic nature of unemployment. Unemployment is a significant effect of illiteracy, as it impedes a nation's progress, resulting in low living standards and an increased crime rate. India is in a critical situation, with nearly 58 percent of unemployed graduates and 77 percent of families without a source of income. About 67 percent receive an annual income of less than 1.2 Lakh per annum. In this situation, India cannot afford to leave any child illiterate, as this would condemn him to a life of unemployment and misery.

Poverty is eradicated
Poverty is on the decline in India, even though it is one of the fastest-growing economies in 2020, and it is one of the most serious consequences of illiteracy. Until 2012, India was known as the country with the greatest number of poor people in the world. Nearly 70.6 million people in India continue to live in abject poverty, and education is the only way to break the vicious cycle of poverty. Higher literacy rates lead to greater access to employment, which in turn leads to better living conditions.

Casteism is eliminated.
In India, the caste system is the world's oldest surviving social hierarchy. Discrimination based on caste stymies India's path to sustainable development. A society that discriminates based on caste, creed, race, religion, or color is enmeshed in a web of hatred, poverty, and insufficient resources. Illiteracy is a factor that contributes to the prevalence of orthodox customs. Education alters one's perception of caste discrimination. It creates a caste-neutral individual with a progressive mindset who wishes to contribute productively to the nation.

Economic Growth and Stability
Education is a critical tool for a country's economic growth and stability. India has twenty million young people who graduate from various disciplines each year and go on to contribute to the country's economic development. Educating youth and adults reveals the hidden potential that can lead India to development.

Improvements to Health and Hygiene
Poor hygienic conditions and health are associated with countries with a high rate of illiteracy. India has moved on from the decades when millions of people died as a result of diseases caused by a lack of healthcare and poor sanitation. To improve the country's health and hygiene sector, schemes such as the "Swachh Bharat Mission," "Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram," and others entice people to come safely under the fold.
